# SALES-PERFORMANCE-REPORT
This sales Performance Report presents a comprehensive analysis of transactional data recorded between January and March 2024, providing a complete, data-driven overview of sales operations across regions, product categories, and sales representativesThe analysis integrates raw dataset exploration with a wide range of Excel-based analytical functions; including SORT, FILTER, SUMIF, SUMIFS, COUNTIF, COUNTIFS, AVERAGEIF, VLOOKUP, XLOOKUP, and INDEX–MATCH, to generate actionable insights that reflect performance trends and fine-grained operational details.
The report evaluates multiple dimensions of sales performance: identifying the top and bottom transactions, examining regional and category-based sales patterns, measuring sales rep contributions, and analyzing chronological and product-specific order behavior. Key computed metrics reveal significant insights such as ₦4,326,860 total sales from Lagos,₦5,564,991 generated by Samuel, ₦9,889,668 from the Tech category, 3,791,501 in Grocery sales for January, and 83 transactions above ₦50,000. Additional metrics ; such as average sales in Grocery (₦150,500.50), average sales by Grace (₦146,013.80), 162 Tech units sold by Samuel, and 27 Tech items sold in February, further enhance understanding of operational efficiency and customer purchasing patterns.
By combining targeted analytical questions with high-level aggregated metrics, this report moves beyond simple data summarization to reveal patterns in customer behavior, high-performing markets, rep productivity, category strength, and the effectiveness of the sales structure. Altogether, it offers a complete and strategic view of sales performance for Q1 2024, supporting informed decision-making, resource optimization, and future planning.

**OBJECTIVES**

The primary objectives of this Sales Performance Report is to transform raw transactional data into clear, actionable insights and provide a strategic overview of sales performance for the period of January–March 2024. Specifically, this report aims to:
1. Evaluate transaction performance
• Identify the highest and lowest sales transactions.
• Assess order quantities and determine patterns in high- and low-volume orders.
2. Analyze product and category behavior
• Determine category-specific trends such as best-selling products
• Identify how frequently key items; such as Tech, Groceries, and Phones, were sold and by whom.
3. Measure sales representative contributions
• Track rep activities such as who handled certain orders, who appears most in specific categories, and who performed best under filtered conditions.
• Use lookups to examine rep-specific metrics (e.g., region, sales values)
4. Examine regional performance
• Identify high-volume regions.
• Determine region-specific patterns, especially when filters and conditions are applied.

5. Perform advanced data lookups for accuracy and auditing
• Retrieve specific order information using VLOOKUP, XLOOKUP, INDEX & MATCH.
• Validate entries using OrderID — based lookups.
• Ensure traceability of sales values, rep names, product categories, and dates.
6. Support strategic decision-making
• Provide insights that can guide resource allocation.
• Highlight operational strengths and areas needing improvement.
• Create an analytical foundation for goal-setting and future sales planning.
